# Company research brief: Blue Origin

Prepared from **Blue Origin’s public website** ([https://www.blueorigin.com/](https://www.blueorigin.com/)), **NASA press materials**, and **reputable news outlets** (see caveats).

## Executive summary

Blue Origin is a U.S. aerospace company founded by **Jeff Bezos** with the stated mission of building a **“road to space for the benefit of Earth,”** emphasizing **reusable rockets**, **American-made engines**, and expansion into **orbital launch (New Glenn)**, **suborbital human flight and research (New Shepard)**, **lunar landers (Blue Moon)**, **commercial LEO destinations (e.g. Orbital Reef with partners)**, and newer initiatives such as **TeraWave** (space-based connectivity) and **Honeybee Robotics** products. Its flagship orbital system, **New Glenn**, is marketed for heavy lift to LEO/GTO with a **reusable first stage** and **7 m fairing**, launched from **LC-36** on the Florida Space Coast. **NASA** selected Blue Origin as a **second Human Landing System provider** (firm-fixed-price award on the order of **~$3.4B** in public reporting) for **sustained lunar landing** alongside other providers. In **late January 2026**, multiple outlets reported that Blue Origin is **pausing crewed New Shepard tourism flights for at least two years** to prioritize **lunar programs** (treat dates and duration as **press-reported** until you confirm an official primary-source statement).

## What the company does

Blue Origin describes itself as building **low-cost, reusable access to space** to unlock **resources and industry in space** while protecting Earth. It develops **vertical takeoff, vertical landing** architectures (**New Shepard** suborbital, **New Glenn** orbital), **BE-4 / BE-3U / BE-7 / BE-3PM** engines and **Blue Thrusters** RCS products, **Blue Moon** lunar lander variants, and partners on **Orbital Reef** as a **commercial LEO space station** concept. It also runs **Club for the Future**, a nonprofit aimed at **STEAM** outreach (linked from Blue Origin’s About page).

## Products and services (public)

- **New Shepard** — Fully reusable **suborbital** system; **6-seat** capsule, large windows, autonomous flight; crosses **Kármán line (~100 km)**; **BE-3PM** LOX/LH₂; strong emphasis on **reuse** and **water-vapor combustion byproduct** claims; **payload** flights for research. Site states **38 missions** and **98 humans** above the Kármán line in BE-3PM section (engines page). **Jan 2026 press:** crewed tourism **paused ≥2 years** (verify officially).
- **New Glenn** — **>320 ft** tall reusable **orbital** rocket; **7 m** fairing; **7× BE-4** on first stage (640,000 lbf sea level each per site), **2× BE-3U** upper stage; **>13 t to GTO**, **45 t to LEO** (site); first stage designed for **minimum 25 flights**; downrange landing on **moving platform** ~620 mi downrange; **LC-36** with **>$1B** rebuild claim (site); partners called out: **NASA ESCAPADE**, **AST SpaceMobile**, **Amazon Leo**.
- **Blue Moon** — **MK1** cargo up to **~3 t** to lunar surface; **MK2** crew/cargo for **sustained lunar presence**; aligned with **NASA** human-rating narrative on site.
- **Engines** — **BE-4** (LOX/LNG, staged combustion, powers New Glenn and **ULA Vulcan** first stage per site), **BE-3U**, **BE-7** (lunar lander), **BE-3PM** (New Shepard), **Blue Thrusters**.
- **Orbital Reef** — Commercial **LEO** space station **with Sierra Space** and other partners; **NASA** has published progress updates on design development and tests.
- **TeraWave** — **Jan 2026** announcement (Blue Origin news + tech press): large **multi-orbit** constellation vision, **very high aggregate throughput** claims, **enterprise/government** positioning; deployment timelines in **press** (e.g. **2027** start mentioned)—treat as **forward-looking**.
- **Honeybee Robotics** — Robotics / motion / exploration products branded under Blue Origin’s homepage navigation.

## Positioning and differentiation

- **Reusability** and **operational cadence** as cost drivers (25+ flights first stage, airline-like ops language).
- **7 m fairing** and **hydrogen upper stage** for **high-energy** missions.
- **American industrial base** — ends reliance on **Russian** RD-class engines narrative for **BE-4** (site).
- **Environmental angle** — LOX/LH₂ for New Shepard → **water vapor** exhaust claim; LNG vs kerosene efficiency narrative for BE-4.
- **Safety culture** — explicit “highest value” messaging on human spaceflight pages.

## Analyst-style assessment

Blue Origin is positioning itself as a **full-stack** U.S. space company: **engines that sell into ULA**, a **heavy reusable orbital launcher** competing for **national and commercial** manifest, **NASA-aligned lunar** hardware, and **adjacent bets** (LEO station, satcom, robotics). **Execution risk** sits in **launch cadence**, **New Glenn booster reuse economics at scale**, **HLS schedule** pressure, and **capital intensity** of constellations and stations. **New Shepard pause** (if confirmed as stated in press) **reallocates** public attention and workforce narrative toward **Moon** and **orbital** businesses.
